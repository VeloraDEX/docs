Velora gives partners two revenue levers on every swap they route: a **partner fee** (a fixed cut in basis points) or a **surplus share** (half of any positive slippage the routing engine or solver network captures). The two are mutually exclusive on a single swap, and the split between partner, user, and Velora differs between Delta and Market.

## How partners earn

- **Partner fee.** A fixed basis-point cut subtracted from the swap output. Predictable per-trade revenue, but it worsens the user-facing quote because the fee comes off the top.
- **Surplus share.** When the route or solver overdelivers vs. the quoted output, that *positive slippage* is the surplus. Partners can claim 50% of it, leaving the user-facing quote untouched. Revenue is variable — it depends on routing edge.

A swap can carry one lever or the other, not both. If a request sets both, the partner fee takes precedence and the surplus flag is ignored.

Requests without a partner identifier fall back to the `anon` partner, which charges a flat 1 bps fee. To unlock the levers above, attach your own `partner` identifier — see [Pro API accounts](/overview/pro-api-accounts).

## Revenue splits at a glance

How fees and surplus are split between the partner, the end user, and Velora:

| Configuration | Delta | Market |
|---|---|---|
| Partner fee, no surplus opt-in* | 100% partner | 85% partner / 15% Velora |
| Surplus share, no fee | 50% Velora / 50% partner or user | 50% Velora / 50% partner or user |
| No fee, no surplus opt-in | 100% Velora | 100% Velora |
| Partner fee + surplus flags both set | Fee wins; surplus flag ignored | Fee wins; surplus flag ignored |

Delta returns 100% of the partner fee to the partner because the protocol funds itself from surplus on the Delta side, not from per-swap fees. On Market, Velora takes a 15% cut of the partner fee to fund routing infrastructure.

<sub>* Tailored arrangements available on request.</sub>

## How it maps to the API

Delta (`GET /quote`) and Market (`GET /prices`, `POST /transactions`) accept the same monetization parameters per request. `isDirectFeeTransfer` is Market-only.

| Parameter<span style={{display: 'inline-block', width: '11rem'}} /> | Type | Default | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| `partner` | string | `anon` | Identifies your integration; ties the request to your partner economics |
| `partnerAddress` | address | — | On-chain recipient of fees or surplus share |
| `partnerFeeBps` | string (bps) | — | Fixed fee in basis points. `"200"` = 2%. Max enforced by your partner tier |
| `takeSurplus` | boolean | `false` | Opt into surplus collection. Requires `partner` or `partnerAddress` |
| `isSurplusToUser` | boolean | `false` | Route surplus to the end user. Requires `takeSurplus: true` |
| `isDirectFeeTransfer`<sup>Market</sup> | boolean | `false` | Bypass the Fee Claimer contract and transfer fees to `partnerAddress` per swap |

<Note>
`partnerFeeBps` and `takeSurplus` are mutually exclusive — set one or the other. The legacy `positiveSlippageToUser` flag is deprecated; use `isSurplusToUser` instead.
</Note>

Pass `partnerFeeBps` to the quote endpoint (`GET /prices` or `GET /quote`) so the fee is reflected in the displayed quote.

## Fee delivery

On **Market**, partner fees accrue inside the **Fee Claimer** contract by default. Partners claim accumulated balances through the Velora Fee Claimer UI, signed by `partnerAddress`. This batches gas across many swaps but adds a claim step. To skip the Fee Claimer and pay fees directly to `partnerAddress` on every swap, set `isDirectFeeTransfer: true` — the per-swap gas cost is higher, but there's nothing to claim.

On **Delta**, fees accrue inside the main Delta contract and partners claim them directly from there — there is no separate Fee Claimer contract.

Surplus routed to the user (`isSurplusToUser: true`) is settled in the same transaction as the swap — no claim step either way.

## How to claim

### Fee Claimer UI

Accumulated balances are claimable from the [Fee Claimer app](https://velora.xyz/fee_claimer). Connect the wallet matching `partnerAddress`, pick the chain, review the available balances per token, and submit a claim transaction. Each chain is claimed separately; gas is paid by the claimer.
